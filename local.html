Our school uses a lot of cups, plastic cups, so students can get access to water. But these cups generate lots of trash, as we don't recycle the cups, but rather throw them in the trash.



On average, our school uses about 3 jugs per day. To get a more precise estimate, we can convert the amount of jugs into cups and use a T-interval

To make a confidence interval, we need to first check conditions:

Representative sample? Yes. The amount of jugs used is collective, and each cup is the same.

Large enough sample? No. But the population of jugs is most likely normal.

Okay to run a 1 sample T-interval, with \(df=19\) and a confidence level of \(95\%\)


Now that our conditions are checked, we can do the mechanics

We don't know the sample or population standard deviation, so we can estimate.

It is reasonable to assume that around half the students at IHS actually use cups, so \(\sigma = 900\)


We are \(95\% \) confident that the true proportion of cups used everyday at IHS is between 3442 and 7914 cups. That is quite a big range, but it makes sense in context because some people may forgot their water bottle one day, one day may be extremely hot. Regardless, even if our school used just under 3500 cups per day, that is a lot!

When Green Team removed plastic cups, many people were angered; but the benefit to environment is undeniable. But there are other alternatives that our school can do to still provide water storage devices to everyone.

Some alternatives may include, fixing the water fountains, providing paper cups, or recycling the cups.

Fixing the water fountains would be an amazing alternative!
