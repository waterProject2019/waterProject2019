Our school uses a lot of cups, plastic cups, so students can get access to water. But these cups generate lots of trash, as we don't recycle the cups, but rather throw them in the trash.



To get 8500 pounds, we computed: 5 grams / 453.592 pound \(\cdot\) 3700 cups \(\cdot\) 200 days per year.


On average, our school uses about 3 jugs per day. To get a more precise estimate, we can convert the amount of jugs into cups and use a T-confidence-interval

The reason why we are using T-interval is because we are looking at the MEAN number of cups used per day.

To make a confidence interval, we need to first check conditions:

What a T-interval is a range of values for which the actual mean number of something can lie.

Our sample is a representative sample becase the amount of jugs used is collective, and each cup is the same.

Our sample is not large enough, but the population of jugs is most likely normal.

Okay to run a 1 sample T-interval, with \(df=19\) and a confidence level of \(95\%\)


Without checking conditions, the result may not be useful, which is why conditions should always be checked before doing any statistical analysis.

A representative sample is needed because then our calculations can be applied to other places.

A large enough sample (or normal population) is required so we can use critical values (explained below)


Now that our conditions are checked, we can do the mechanics

Where \(\mu_{\bar{x}}\) represents the sample mean number of cups

We don't know the sample or population standard deviation, so we can estimate. Because green team took away the cups, it is impossible for us to get a value for the sample sd, thus an estimate is the best we can do.

It is reasonable to assume that around half the students at IHS actually use cups, so \(s = 800\) where \(s\) represents the sample standard deviation.

What SE represents is the standard error. This measures a range that the true number of cups can lie between.

\(t^{*} \) represents the critical value corresponding to a different confidence level. df, or degrees of freedom, is just a way to determine the shape of a t-distribution.


We are \(95\% \) confident that the true proportion of cups used everyday at IHS is between 3690 and 7665 cups. That is quite a big range, but it makes sense in context because some people may forgot their water bottle one day, one day may be extremely hot. Regardless, even if our school used just under 3500 cups per day, that is a lot!

But why do we need to consider alternatives to plastic storage?

This is because plastic has many harmful effects on the environment and animals. These can include:

Toxin Release Gene expression
Bisphenol A (BPA) and PS oligomer are both released as plastic decomposes. BPA and PS oligomer causes additional pollution; in addition, they can disrupt hormone function and can affect the reproductive system. These toxins can also disrupt the endocrine system. Plastic has been seen to drive animals away from the best adaptation. Relating to toxins, the toxins can be consumed by organisms (BPA is water soluble, and can leach into food and drink). This causes a disruption in the genes which can stay to the fourth generation, or even further! But how do the toxins affect the genes? This is still being discovered, but what is important is that plastic dissolving does affect gene expression.
