But why do we need to consider alternatives to plastic storage?

This is because plastic has many harmful effects on the environment and animals. These can include:

Toxin Release Gene expression
Bisphenol A (BPA) and PS oligomer are both released as plastic decomposes. BPA and PS oligomer causes additional pollution; in addition, they can disrupt hormone function and can affect the reproductive system. These toxins can also disrupt the endocrine system. Plastic has been seen to drive animals away from the best adaptation. Relating to toxins, the toxins can be consumed by organisms (BPA is water soluble, and can leach into food and drink). This causes a disruption in the genes which can stay to the fourth generation, or even further! But how do the toxins affect the genes? This is still being discovered, but what is important is that plastic dissolving does affect gene expression.

Q: How much plastic did our school use, what are the effects, and what can we do to minimize use?


Based off of calculations and rough estimates, our school (being conservative) used to use about 3700 cups a day. This calculation was made using a a t-interval and estimating that around 3 jugs are used per day. But why does using this many plastic cups matter? Aren’t they recyclable?

Plastic has a profound effect on the environment; these can include, and definitely are not limited to, polluting areas, and releasing toxins that eventually lead to gene expression changes in organisms that come in contact with the toxins. Our school contributes to this by simply not properly disposing of plastic cups. The “recycling” bins that are seen outside the water jugs are in reality trash bins; whatever goes in makes its way to the trash can. Now imagine 3700 cups going into the trash every day!

To expand upon effects of plastic, one major consequence is the formation of an ocean of plastic. In the oceans right now, there are millions of tons of plastic alone. This ruins the habitats for marine life, and lots of this plastic gets washed up on shore onto beaches and other coastline attractions.

Plastic also releases toxins as it decomposes. But the scale of toxins released is tremendous! Plastic takes hundreds, or even thousands, of years to decompose, and toxins are constantly leaking out. These toxins find themselves in food and water of many organisms (including humans). This leads to damaging of vital body systems, and different gene expressions, that goes in the opposite way of evolution. But many generations after yours, the effects will still be present.

One alternative that our school can do to lower plastic use is to open the water fountains. This could eliminate the usage of all cups! The only downside is that opening all the fountains will be a hassle, and if the water is lead poisoned, then lead-water would be circulating throughout.

Another alternative could be use paper cups! Most paper cups are biodegradable, environmentally friendly, and are relatively cheap. One issue with using paper cups is that some types of paper cups come with a plastic lining on the inside of the cup, which complicates recycling them.

One simple (but not so simple) solution could be to recycle the cups rather than throw them in the trash. This would ensure no plastic waste, as all the plastic would be recycled. We are lucky in Tompkins County to have a recycling center. Some less fortunate places would have trouble recycling, as recycling facilities are needed and are expensive to maintain.

Any one of these alternatives would lead to a more environmentally friendly school system, and minimize plastic waste. Minimizing plastic waste would lead to a healthier ecosystem and a cleaner world. These small steps toward a cleaner future can be a model for others to contribute to the well-being of our world.
